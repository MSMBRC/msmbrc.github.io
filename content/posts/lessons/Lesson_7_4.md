# Lesson 7.4 – The Great Train Robbery

## Argomenti
1. Past Simple – domande aperte e chiuse
2. Object Questions vs Subject Questions
3. Phrasal Verbs

---

## 1. Past Simple – Domande

### Domande chiuse → Did + soggetto + infinito

*"Did she drive to work?"* → *Yes, she did. / No, she didn't.*

### Domande aperte → Question word + did + soggetto + infinito

*"Where did you go?"* / *"What did they eat?"*

### Pronuncia del suffisso -ed nei verbi regolari

| Pronuncia | Quando | Esempi |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **/id/** | Verbo finisce in -d o -t | attend→attended, act→acted |
| **/t/ o /d/** | Tutti gli altri | work→worked, play→played |

---

## 2. Object Questions vs Subject Questions

### Object Question
La particola interrogativa chiede l'**oggetto** della frase. Si usa **did**.

| Domanda | Risposta |
|---------|---------|
| *What did you read?* | *I read a magazine.* |
| *Who did she see?* | *She saw Richard.* |

### Subject Question
La particola interrogativa chiede il **soggetto**. **Non si usa did** → direttamente Past Simple.

| Domanda | Risposta |
|---------|---------|
| *Who called you?* | *Jenny called me.* |
| *Who ate the cake?* | *Laura ate the cake.* |
| *What damaged the roof?* | *The wind damaged the roof.* |

> **Regola:** se la domanda con *who/what* chiede **chi fa** l'azione → Subject Question → no *did*.
