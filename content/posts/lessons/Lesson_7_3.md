# Lesson 7.3 – Have You Ever?

## Argomenti
1. Present Perfect per le esperienze
2. Present Perfect + Past Simple
3. Too / Either

---

## 1. Present Perfect – Esperienze

**Quando si usa:** esperienze della vita **senza specificare quando**.

**Forma:** soggetto + **have/has** + Past Participle

| Soggetto | Affermativa | Negativa | Domanda |
|----------|-------------|----------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | have broken | haven't broken | Have … broken? |
| He / She / It | has broken | hasn't broken | Has … broken? |

**Con "ever" e "never":**
- *Have you **ever** been to Australia?* → Sei mai stato in Australia?
- *I have **never** broken a law.* → Non ho mai infranto una legge.

### Past Participle – verbi irregolari comuni

| Infinito | Past Simple | Past Participle | Italiano |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| be | was/were | been | essere |
| break | broke | broken | rompere |
| drink | drank | drunk | bere |
| drive | drove | driven | guidare |
| fly | flew | flown | volare |
| meet | met | met | incontrare |
| sleep | slept | slept | dormire |

---

## 2. Present Perfect + Past Simple

Il **Present Perfect** introduce l'esperienza, il **Past Simple** aggiunge i dettagli (quando, come).

*"I have been to Australia once."* → esperienza
*"I went there two years ago."* → dettaglio specifico

---

## 3. Too / Either

| Situazione | Parola | Esempio |
|------------|--------|---------|
| Frase **positiva** → "anch'io" | **too** | *"I've drunk tequila, too."* |
| Frase **negativa** → "neanche io" | **either** | *"I haven't broken a bone either."* |
