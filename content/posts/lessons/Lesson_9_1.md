## Argomenti
Passivo in tutti i tempi principali

---

## Schema generale del Passivo

**Regola:** oggetto dell'azione diventa soggetto della frase passiva. Si usa quando l'agente è sconosciuto o non importante.

---

## Tabella riassuntiva dei passivi

| Tempo | Forma | Esempio |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Present Simple** | soggetto + is/are + Past Participle | *The building is finished.* |
| **Past Simple** | soggetto + was/were + Past Participle | *The bridges were built.* |
| **Present Perfect** | soggetto + have/has been + Past Participle | *The furniture has been thrown away.* |
| **Modal** | soggetto + modal + be + Past Participle | *The project must be completed.* |
| **Future (will)** | soggetto + will be + Past Participle | *The tram line will be built.* |
| **Future (going to)** | soggetto + going to be + Past Participle | *The car is going to be driven by my brother.* |
| **Present Continuous** | soggetto + is/are being + Past Participle | *The rooms are being refurbished.* |
| **Past Continuous** | soggetto + was/were being + Past Participle | *The roof was being fixed when I arrived.* |

---

## Note importanti

- L'agente si specifica con **by**: *"It will be built by 10 builders."*
- I **semi-modali** funzionano anche al passivo: *"The project has got to be finished by tomorrow."*
- Il **Present Continuous passivo** può indicare anche un piano futuro: *"The theme park is being opened in 2040."*
- Con il **passivo modale**, il significato del modale rimane invariato: *"The project might not be finished on time."*
