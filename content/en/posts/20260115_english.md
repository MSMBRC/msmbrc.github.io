# English lesson index

Lessons from the English course I have been attending since around 2022. Each level corresponds to a stage of the journey, with the relevant key lessons linked below.

---

## Level 5
Introduction to progressive tenses and the first structures for talking about the present, near future and past. We start describing ongoing actions, contrasting habits with momentary situations, and talking about the past using the verb "to be".

- [**Lesson 5.1**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_5_1) – Present Continuous and Adverbs of Manner
- [**Lesson 5.2**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_5_2) – Present Simple vs Present Continuous, clothing vocabulary
- [**Lesson 5.3**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_5_3) – Future with Present Continuous and Present Simple, telling the time and dates
- [**Lesson 5.4**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_5_4) – Quantifiers (some/any, much/many), food vocabulary
- [**Lesson 5.5**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_5_5) – Past Simple of "to be", there was/were

---

## Level 6
Expanding into comparison, the past and the future. We learn to compare people and things, recount past events, and organise future plans with more precise structures.

- [**Lesson 6.1**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_6_1) – Comparatives, modifiers much/a bit/as…as
- [**Lesson 6.2**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_6_2) – Superlatives, means of transport and prepositions by/on
- [**Lesson 6.3**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_6_3) – Future Simple (will) for spontaneous decisions, making and responding to suggestions
- [**Lesson 6.4**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_6_4) – Past Simple regular and irregular verbs, questions in the past tense
- [**Lesson 6.5**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_6_5) – Past Continuous, storytelling with Past Continuous + Past Simple, when/while

---

## Level 7
Consolidating the past and introducing the Present Perfect. We work on the future with "going to", modal verbs, personal experiences and more complex questions.

- [**Lesson 7.1**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_7_1) – "Going to" for intentions and predictions, comparison of the 3 future forms
- [**Lesson 7.2**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_7_2) – Modal verbs (may, might, could, should, must), giving advice
- [**Lesson 7.3**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_7_3) – Present Perfect for experiences (ever/never), Present Perfect + Past Simple, too/either
- [**Lesson 7.4**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_7_4) – Open and closed questions in the Past Simple, Object vs Subject Questions, phrasal verbs
- [**Lesson 7.5**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_7_5) – Quantifiers (too/too much/too many, enough, plenty of), venues and events vocabulary

---

## Level 8
A deeper look at the Present Perfect in all its nuances, introduction to the passive voice and advanced modal verbs. Language becomes more precise and articulate.

- [**Lesson 8.1**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_8_1) – Present Perfect with since/for, already/yet, Present Perfect vs Past Simple
- [**Lesson 8.2**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_8_2) – Present Perfect with just, giving news, Present Perfect Continuous
- [**Lesson 8.3**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_8_3) – Present and Past Passive, passive in the news, crime vocabulary
- [**Lesson 8.4**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_8_4) – Must vs Have To, Mustn't vs Don't Have To, customs and obligations
- [**Lesson 8.5**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_8_5) – Semi-modals (ought to, had better, have got to, might as well), giving opinions

---

## Level 9
Conditionals and the passive across all tenses. We start building hypothetical reasoning, talking about childhood and change, and planning with precision.

- [**Lesson 9.1**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_9_1) – Passive in all tenses (present, past, perfect, modal, future, continuous)
- [**Lesson 9.2**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_9_2) – Phrasal verbs for relationships, "used to" and "would" for the past
- [**Lesson 9.3**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_9_3) – Phrasal verbs related to progress, Zero Conditional, First Conditional
- [**Lesson 9.4**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_9_4) – Second Conditional, 1st vs 2nd comparison, subjunctive with "were"
- [**Lesson 9.5**](/../../posts/lessons/Lesson_9_5) – Revision of the 3 future forms, making plans and polite requests
